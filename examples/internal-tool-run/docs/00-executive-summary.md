# DeployGate — Executive Summary

**The idea.** An internal tool where engineers request production deployments and team leads approve or reject them, leaving a tamper-evident audit trail of who shipped what and who signed off.

**The problem.** Production deploys happen ad hoc over Slack DMs — no consistent approval step and no record of who authorized a release. That gap bites hardest during incident review, when nobody can say who signed off.

**Who it's for.**
- Engineer (expert) — requests a deploy of a service to an environment
- Team lead (expert) — approves or rejects deploy requests for their services

## What we're building (MVP)
- **Request a deploy** — an engineer queues a service/env for approval instead of shipping silently · serves g1
- **Approve or reject** — a lead decides with a reason; the requester may not approve their own · serves g1
- **Audit trail view** — browse the history of requests and decisions · serves g2

_Deliberately deferred:_ Notifications, Per-service approver policies — the core request→decide→record loop delivers g1 and g2 and moves s1 to 100% on its own.

## Why it will work / how we'll know
- Every production deploy passes through an explicit approval step (g1)
- A durable audit trail records requester, approver, service, and decision (g2)
- Approvals stay fast enough not to slow the team down (g3)

**Success =** 100% of production deploys went through an approval (s1) · median request→decision under 30 minutes (s2)

## Top risks
1. **An engineer could approve their own deploy** — SSO authenticates identity but does not authorize approval; enforce requester ≠ approver + an approver allowlist server-side.
2. **Audit records could be altered to hide who authorized a release** — append-only audit table with UPDATE/DELETE revoked at the DB grant level.
3. **Slow approvals push engineers to route around the tool, leaving audit gaps** — one-screen request flow; prioritize notifications in Next.

## Open decisions requiring your input
- **Where should the approver allowlist come from?** — options: source automatically from GitHub team membership (less admin, drifts with the org chart) · maintain an explicit list inside DeployGate (more control, needs upkeep). (Security challenge on brief.assumptions.a1)
