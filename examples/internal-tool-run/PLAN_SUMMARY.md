# DeployGate — Plan Summary

**What we're building.** An internal tool where engineers request production deployments and team leads approve or reject them, leaving a tamper-evident audit trail of who shipped what and who signed off.

**Who it's for.** Engineers (request a deploy) · Team leads (approve or reject for their services).

## MVP feature list
- **Request a deploy** — engineer queues a service/env for approval (serves g1)
- **Approve or reject** — lead decides with a reason; requester may not approve their own (serves g1)
- **Audit trail view** — browse requests + decisions for incident review (serves g2)

_Deferred to roadmap:_ Notifications (Next), Per-service approver policies (Later) — the core request→decide→record loop delivers g1 + g2 and hits 100% on s1 without them.

## Timeline & approach
Single TypeScript/Express service behind the existing SSO gateway; Postgres for state + an append-only audit trail; approval calls the existing deploy pipeline (it does not deploy anything itself). Timeline: 1–3 months.
Roadmap: **MVP** approval loop + audit → **Next** notifications (drives median approval under 30 min) → **Later** per-service approver policies.

## Top 3 risks
1. **An engineer could approve their own deploy** (SSO authenticates but doesn't authorize) — enforce requester ≠ approver + an approver allowlist server-side.
2. **Audit records could be altered** — append-only table with UPDATE/DELETE revoked at the DB grant; no app path to mutate.
3. **Slow approvals cause bypass** (audit gaps) — one-screen request flow; prioritize notifications in Next.
